  • Figure 3Estimated proportion of subjects with depression at each age according to level of self-report depression scores
    The legend indicates three levels of MFQ self-report scores, 20, 30 and 50, from a possible range of 0 to 66, obtained from 1056 girls aged 11–16 years. The estimated proportion of cases (y axis) is, as expected, greater for higher scores at each age. Importantly the same level of symptom scores at each age estimates significantly different proportions of cases. This suggests developmentally sensitive differences in adolescent girls for the liability for detecting depression from self-reports. [Data first published in Cooper & Goodyer (1993), pp. 369–374].
